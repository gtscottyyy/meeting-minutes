As the chairman of the Goshen Township Board of Trustees, throughout 2024, it is my privilege to present the state of the township address. Over the past year, we made considerable progress in advancing our community, upgrading our infrastructure, and safeguarding the well-being of our residents. One of the most significant achievements occurred when the Board of Trustees, Fire, EMS Chief Myers, and Administrator Rose provided testimony to both the Ohio House and Senate. With the strong support of Representative Jean Schmidt, we successfully secured funding for the demolition and reconstruction of Fire Station 18. The station, built in 1982, suffered severe damage, including a partial collapse caused by a tornado that devastated our community on July 6, 2022. The new fire station construction does not require local tax dollars, as it is funded by our insurance company, the Ohio Emergency Management Agency, and other state funding. The new fire department will incorporate state-of-the-art features, prioritizing the health and safety of our firefighters, and it is designed to serve our community effectively and efficiently for many decades to come. Goshen Township also received just over $1.7 million in 2022 from the Federal American Rescue Plan, which is known as ARPA. With the deadline of December 31, 2024, we needed to allocate those funds, and I am proud of this Board's spending decisions made to benefit Goshen Township and support the services utilized by our residents. We allocated these funds carefully with thorough and deliberate consideration and planning. Paving projects completed in the fall of 2023, with a total cost of $831,940, represent a significant milestone. This undertaking is believed to be the largest township paving project in the history of Goshen Township, with more than 70 percent of the funding secured through grants and supplemented with ARPA funds. As a result, we were able to enhance our road infrastructure without imposing a substantial financial burden on our residents. Last year, several projects were undertaken to improve our administrative and police facilities as well. The parking lot was sealed and striped, and the sidewalks leading to the building were repaired. Furthermore, new flooring was installed throughout both the upper and lower levels, and these enhancements have created a fresh and welcoming environment for all visitors. Our infrastructure improvements continued with the construction of a new 30 by 80 service garage, replacement of a service garage roof, and the addition of essential pieces of equipment, including a service one-ton truck, as Chad just mentioned, with a snowplow and also a new lawnmower. We also added a new police cruiser to our fleet, ensuring our officers have the tools they need to keep our community safe.
